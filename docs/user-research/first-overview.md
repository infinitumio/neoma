# First-user overview — question set

A script for sitting with an early user as they try neoma for the first time, so we
can decide what to change or add. This is a living document — edit questions, drop ones
that don't fit, and add product-specific probes as neoma evolves.

## How to run it

- **Time:** ~30–45 minutes.
- **Format:** moderated. Ask them to **think aloud** while they work.
- **Watch, don't rescue:** note *where they hesitate*, not just what they say. When they
  ask "how do I…?", reply "what would you try?" before answering.
- **Task vs. opinion:** items marked _Task_ are things to watch them attempt — you learn
  more from where they get stuck than from their stated opinions. Keep opinion questions
  open-ended and non-leading.
- **Separate "nice idea" from "I'd use it":** question 33 forces that distinction — don't
  skip it.

---

## A. Background (2 min — frames all their answers)

1. What do you currently use for notes / research journaling? What do you like and dislike
   about it?
2. Are you a researcher, student, developer, or something else — and what would you mainly
   use neoma for?
3. How important to you is it that your notes stay as plain files you fully own? Why?

## B. First impressions (before you explain anything)

4. Looking at the welcome screen — in your own words, what is this app and who is it for?
5. What stands out first, and does anything look confusing or untrustworthy?
6. From the four options (browser vault, local folder, import, demo) — which would you
   click first, and why?

## C. Getting started (task-based)

7. _Task:_ "Set yourself up so you can start writing." — Which path did they pick? Did
   "browser vault" vs "local folder" make sense without help?
8. Did the privacy line ("no account… stays on this device") land, or did you skip past it?
9. _Task:_ "Open the demo vault and get a feel for it." — What did they explore first?

## D. Writing & editing

10. _Task:_ "Create a note and write a few lines with a heading, bold text, and a list."
11. Did you notice the Edit / Split / Reading / **Source** view switcher? Was it clear what
    each does, and which would you use day-to-day?
12. _Task:_ "Select some text and make it bold using the toolbar that appears." — Was the
    floating selection toolbar discoverable/useful, or intrusive?
13. Did anything about saving worry you? (Did they notice the "Saved" indicator / trust
    that work isn't lost?)

## E. Linking, tags, backlinks, graph

14. _Task:_ "Link this note to another one." — Did they discover `[[ ]]` and the
    autocomplete on their own?
15. _Task:_ "Find everything that links back to this note." — Was the backlinks panel
    findable and clear?
16. _Task:_ "Open the graph." — Was it useful or just decorative? Would you actually use it?
17. Did tags (`#tag`) behave the way you expected?

## F. Search

18. _Task:_ "Find a note that mentions [a specific word]." — Did they find search? Were
    results fast and clear?
19. Did you try any filters (phrases, `tag:`, dates)? Did you expect them to exist?

## G. Research workflow (the differentiator)

20. _Task:_ "Start today's journal entry." — Did the daily-note flow (and the
    confirm-before-create step) make sense?
21. Look at the built-in templates (literature note, experiment log, supervisor meeting,
    research question). Do these match how you actually work? What's missing or wrong?
22. Do the citation features (`[@key]`, DOI/BibTeX fields) matter to you, or is that noise?

## H. Storage, portability & trust

23. Was the difference between a browser vault and a local folder clear — and did the
    warning that clearing browser data deletes a browser vault worry or reassure you?
24. _Task:_ "Export your vault, then imagine opening these notes in another app." — Did
    export feel trustworthy? Did you believe your data is truly portable?
25. On a scale of 1–5, how much do you trust that neoma isn't sending your notes anywhere?
    What would raise that number?

## I. Offline & install

26. _Task (if possible):_ "Go offline and reload." — Did it keep working? Was the "Offline"
    indicator reassuring rather than alarming?
27. Would you install it as an app? What would make you want to?

## J. Performance, responsive, accessibility

28. Did anything feel slow, janky, or laggy at any point?
29. _(If relevant)_ Try it on your phone/tablet — does it hold up?
30. _(If they use one)_ Did keyboard-only navigation or a screen reader work for you?

## K. Overall & prioritization

31. What was the single most confusing or frustrating moment?
32. What one thing would make you choose this over your current tool — and what one missing
    thing would stop you?
33. Would you use neoma next week for real work? If not, what's the blocker?
34. If you could add or change three things, what would they be (ranked)?
35. Would you recommend it to a colleague? Why / why not?
