# Privacy Policy

_Last updated: 24 July 2026_

Neoma is built to keep your knowledge private by default. The short version:
**the Neoma application collects nothing about you and sends nothing anywhere.**

## The application

Whether you use the web app, the desktop app (macOS, Windows, Linux) or the
iOS app, Neoma runs entirely on your own device:

- **No account.** There is no sign-up, no login, and no email is ever requested.
- **No telemetry.** Neoma contains no analytics, tracking, crash reporting or
  usage collection. This is verified in our continuous-integration checks,
  which fail the build if a reference to a third-party origin appears.
- **No cloud.** Your notes are stored locally — as plain Markdown files in a
  folder you choose, or in your browser's local storage. They are never
  uploaded to us or to anyone else.
- **Offline by design.** The app boots and works with no network connection.

Because your notes never leave your device, we have no access to them and could
not read, share or sell them even if we wanted to.

## Your data

Your notes, vaults and settings are yours. They live on your device (and, if you
chose a local folder, as ordinary files you already control). You can back them
up, move them, or delete them at any time using your normal file tools — there
is nothing to "export" because nothing was ever locked away.

## This website

This marketing website (`neomadev.app`) is a set of static pages served by our
hosting provider. We do not add analytics or tracking cookies to it. Our hosting
provider may automatically record standard technical request logs (such as IP
address and user-agent) for security and reliability, as described in their own
privacy policy. Downloads of the desktop apps are served by GitHub from the
project's Releases page and are subject to GitHub's privacy policy.

## Third-party services

Neoma does not embed third-party trackers, advertising, fonts or scripts. The
only external interactions are ones you explicitly initiate — for example,
clicking a download link that takes you to GitHub.

## Changes

If this policy changes, the "last updated" date above will change with it. Since
